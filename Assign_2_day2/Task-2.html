Chapter 1: Vadapav

Vada Pav is a famous Mumbai street food made of a spicy potato fritter sandwiched in a pav (bread bun). It is often served with chutneys and fried green chilies, and is popularly called the Indian burger.

Chapter 2: Bhaji Pav

Bhaji Pav, commonly known as Pav Bhaji, is a spicy mashed vegetable curry served with butter-toasted pav. It is one of the most loved Indian street foods, especially in Mumbai.

Chapter 3: Samosa Pav

Samosa Pav is a crunchy snack where a hot, crispy fried samosa is stuffed inside a pav along with tangy and spicy chutneys, making it a quick and flavorful street food.

Chapter 4: Idli

Idli is a soft and fluffy steamed rice cake from South India. It is healthy, light, and typically served with coconut chutney and sambar.

Chapter 5: Medu Vada

Medu Vada is a popular South Indian snack shaped like a doughnut, made from urad dal batter and deep-fried until crispy. It is often paired with chutney and sambar.

TOP

Chapter 6: Kanda Bhaji

Kanda Bhaji, or onion fritters, are crispy pakoras made with onions and gram flour. They are especially enjoyed with hot tea during rainy days.

TOP

Chapter 7: Pattice

Pattice is a shallow-fried or deep-fried potato patty, sometimes stuffed with peas and spices. It is a tasty snack often eaten with chutneys or pav.

TOP

Chapter 8: Dhokla

Dhokla is a soft, spongy, steamed Gujarati snack made from fermented chickpea flour. It is tempered with mustard seeds and garnished with coriander and coconut.

TOP

Chapter 9: Panipuri

Panipuri is a very popular Indian street snack consisting of crispy hollow puris filled with spicy tangy water, mashed potatoes, chickpeas, and chutneys.

TOP

Chapter 10: Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is a staple South Indian dish served with coconut chutney and sambar.
